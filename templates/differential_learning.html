Differential Learning Fieldwork

  • Fieldwork
  • Key Facts
  • Fieldwork Organizations

  • The Fieldwork programme is an integral part of the HRM & LR curriculum and provides opportunities for students to reflect, apply and integrate acquired skills to work situations in organizations. It thereby serves as an interface between theory and practice.

    The HRM & LR students are placed in four different organizations, representing different industry sectors, during the two year duration of their programme. The fieldwork programme comprises of the following -

    1. Concurrent placement - In the first three semesters, students attend fieldwork in three different organizations, twice a week
    2. Corporate block placement - In the final semester, students undertake a month long block fieldwork placement in an organization
    3. NGO block placement - At the end of the fourth semester, the students work in a Non governmental organization for one month

  • The programme is overseen by the Dean of the School of Management and Labour Studies and by the School Board. Currently, the school deals with more than 100 organizations with respect to fieldwork. The fieldwork curriculum and practice is regularly revised and timely inputs are solicited from industry experts.

    The students are expected to record their learnings and to compare actual business practices with theory. Focus group discussions as well as individualized feedback sessions are conducted on a weekly basis with a faculty mentor.
